Why does life start feeling like it's passing by?

You're not online too much. You're just not noticing enough in between.

Research shows the brain only encodes vivid, lasting memories during moments of genuine attention — when something catches your eye, moves you, or makes you pause. Routine moments on autopilot, including passive scrolling, largely don't make it in. This is why whole weeks can disappear without a single thing you can point to and say: that was good.

Nowhere gives you one small prompt before you go online. Not a limit. Not a lecture. Just a moment to look up — and let something real in first.

That's the part that stays with you.

Hey — I'm Kosta. I built Nowhere solo — one person, seven months, no prior dev background. No investors, no team, no compromises on the vision.

It started with a personal observation: every day was unique, but at the end I couldn't tell you what made it mine. So I built the thing I needed — still the only tool I've found that doesn't treat screens as the enemy.

Nowhere isn't about less screen time. It's about noticing your day holds more than screens.
